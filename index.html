About Me

Doctoral Researcher in Computer Engineering developing battery-free sensing systems and energy-efficient embedded computing. My research focuses on designing sensors that operate without batteries by harvesting ambient energy and using ultra-low-power circuits to detect, process, and transmit data. This work includes paper-based wireless sensors with long-range communication and embedded systems that perform motion recognition directly at the sensor.

A lead contributor to peer-reviewed research published at leading international venues, including an ACM IMWUT Distinguished Paper Award, with an associated patent pending and institutional news coverage. Seeking an academic or national laboratory research career in energy-efficient sensing and embedded systems.

NeuroCamTags (ACM IMWUT 2024)

Developed battery-free sensing tags using ambient-light harvesting and high-frequency LED modulation. Designed event-based pipelines for simultaneous localization and identification of multiple tags. Demonstrated room- and floor-scale sensing at ranges up to 200 ft.
